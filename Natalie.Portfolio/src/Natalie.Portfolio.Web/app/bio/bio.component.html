Bio

About Natalie

Natalie Bechmann enjoys solo playing, frequently partaking in concerts and competitions. She earned a place at the Royal Northern College of Music with the Haydn Cello Concerto in C major and Martinu’s Variations of a Slovak Theme. In the past month, she played Fauré’s Papillon in the RNCM Salon Prize and put on a New Year’s Day concert in Felixstowe, Suffolk, playing Haydn’s Cello Concerto in D major and the Beethoven Cello Sonata in A major. She also acquired a distinction in her ABRSM Diploma in 2015 as well as her Grade 8 in 2013. For her ABRSM Diploma, she played Chopin’s Sonata for Cello and Piano in G minor, Bach’s Cello Suite in C major and Martinu’s Variations on a Theme of Rossini.

As an enthusiast of chamber music, Natalie plays in several ensembles. In the past, she has had experience of professional engagements with the Avocet Quartet. She now competes regularly in the RNCM Chamber Music prizes with the Obsidian, Arc and Èmbechet Piano Trios. As part of these trios, she has particularly enjoyed playing the Lalo Piano Trio in C minor and Beethoven, ‘Ghost’. She also enjoys pop crossover music with the Trillo Cello Trio. She frequently arranges music of different genres for Trillo.

Orchestral playing in another passion of Natalie’s. At the RNCM, she has been on front desk of the RNCM Concert Orchestra, playing Brahms’ Academic Festival and Tragic Overtures, and was the principal of a Swing Orchestra gig on Manchester Day 2016. She also has experience playing new compositions with the RNCM Brand New Orchestra. During her time in Suffolk, she was principal cellist of the Suffolk Youth Orchestra, notably playing Britten’s War Requiem, Elgar’s ‘Enigma’ Variations, Shostakovich’s Symphony No. 5 and Vaughan Williams’ A London Symphony. She was also a frequent player in the Kingfisher and Wolsey Orchestras, particularly enjoying the concert of Beethoven’s Symphony No.9 and Mozart’s Requiem at the world-renowned concert hall, Snape Maltings.
