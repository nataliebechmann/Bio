About Natalie

Natalie Bechmann grew up part of a musical family in Suffolk, England. From an early age, she harboured a passion for music and started the cello aged 5. Throughout her childhood, she was taught by soloist and ex-member of the Ulster Orchestra, Susanna Graham. Regular lessons with her enabled Natalie to take part in the Suffolk County Music scene. She was part of South Suffolk Young Strings, South Suffolk Youth Orchestra and Suffolk Youth Orchestra for many years, becoming principal cellist of all three. She also competed regularly in the Suffolk Festival, earning awards for Best Recital, String Champion and Suffolk Chamber Players in 2014. At this point she also began teaching both cello and piano, coaching a range of different ages and standards. She then began learning with international soloist and teacher at Trinity Laban College of Music, Naomi Butterworth, and secured a place at the RNCM. Now in second year, she studies with the Hallé principal of cello and soloist, Nicholas Trygstad. At the RNCM, Natalie has taken an interest in many different areas of music, including classical chamber groups, orchestras and solos, plus collaborative pop crossover projects. Read More.

ABRSM DIPLOMA

Ensembles

Natalie plays regularly with several chamber music groups, including Trillo Cello Trio, Èmbechet Piano Trio and Arc Piano Trio. All three groups were founded at the RNCM. Both piano trios compete regularly at the RNCM, playing pieces such as Schumann’s Piano Trio No. 3 and Beethoven’s ‘Ghost’. Arc Piano Trio was created in 2016, and includes Nastasia Loucaidou on piano and Raye Harvey on cello. The Èmbechet Piano Trio was established in early 2017, involving Laura Embrey on violin and Mackenzie Paget on piano. Trillo is a Pop-Classical crossover group comprised of fellow second year cellists Reyan Murtadha and Rachel Newbold. They frequently do low-key performances of their repertoire, such as busking in the Manchester City Centre. Crowd favourites include Adele’s Rolling in the Deep, Coldplay’s Magic and their Game of Thrones Season 5 medley. All their repertory is arranged or composed by members of the group.
